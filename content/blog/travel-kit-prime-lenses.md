The best travel kit is the one you actually bring. I've owned zooms that covered every focal length and left them at the hotel because they were too heavy to justify for a walking day. This is what I carry instead.

## Background

Six years on Sony APS-C, then a jump to full-frame two years ago. Every system change reshuffled the kit. The current three-lens setup has survived twelve trips without me wishing I'd packed differently.

## The three lenses

### 35mm f/1.8 — the workhorse

This is on the camera 70% of the time. On full-frame it's wide enough to capture a scene but tight enough to feel deliberate. Fast enough for evening street shooting without pushing ISO past 3200.

### 85mm f/1.8 — portraits and compression

Every city has a moment where you want to isolate a subject from a busy background. The 85 does this better than any other focal length in the kit, and the f/1.8 bokeh is smooth enough that I've never felt I needed the f/1.4 version.

### 20mm f/1.8 — architecture and interiors

The wide prime earns its weight on trips with notable architecture or tight interiors. Rectilinear, fast, and small enough that it doesn't unbalance the bag.

### Trade-offs

- No zoom means committing to a focal length before raising the camera — this is a feature, not a bug; it slows me down in the right way
- Three primes weigh less than a standard 24-70 f/2.8 zoom
- The gap between 35 and 85 occasionally hurts; I've missed a few shots at 50mm equivalent that I should have had

## What I left behind

A 135mm f/1.8 that I love for portraits but almost never use when traveling. A 12-24mm zoom that's brilliant for landscapes but adds 600g for a capability I use twice a year.

## What's next

I'm keeping an eye on a compact 50mm that would fill the gap between my 35 and 85. Might complete the kit without meaningfully changing the bag weight.
